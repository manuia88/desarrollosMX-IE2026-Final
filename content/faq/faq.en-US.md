# Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about DesarrollosMX, our real estate intelligence indices, and how we work.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-24

## What is DesarrollosMX?

DesarrollosMX is a real estate intelligence platform for Latin America, with localized access for U.S. users interested in the region. We consolidate public data, market information, and proprietary models to provide indices, analytics, and tools that help you better understand a neighborhood, city, or development before making real estate decisions. We combine quantitative data with clear narratives for non-specialists.

## Who can use the platform?

Anyone over 18 years of age with internet access. It is designed for three profiles: individuals exploring areas to buy, rent, or invest; investors evaluating profitability and trends; and real estate professionals (agents, developers, consultants) seeking market intelligence. Registration is free in the initial version.

## How much does it cost?

During the initial phase (H1), basic access is free. We are preparing subscription plans with advanced features (deep comparative analytics, exports, API access) to be launched in later phases. There will always be a free tier allowing exploration of core features. Final pricing will be communicated before commercial launch.

## What is a DMX index?

A DMX index is a composite metric that synthesizes multiple variables about an area or neighborhood into a single, easy-to-interpret number. For example, it combines historical appreciation, nearby amenities, connectivity, safety, and other factors to produce a comparative score. Each index has public documentation explaining its component variables and calculation method.

## What is the Neighborhood Genome?

The Neighborhood Genome is a visual representation of a neighborhood's "DNA": what makes it unique, how its population is composed, what types of properties predominate, what services it offers, and how it compares to similar areas. Like biological DNA, it combines multiple components that together define each area's distinctive identity.

## What is the Climate Twin?

The Climate Twin feature identifies neighborhoods or areas with characteristics similar to another one you are interested in, but in other cities or countries. If you enjoy living in Condesa (Mexico City), the Climate Twin suggests comparable areas in Guadalajara, Buenos Aires, São Paulo, or Miami. Similarity combines urban fabric, demographics, services, and general atmosphere — not just climate.

## What are Ghost Zones?

Ghost Zones are areas showing anomalous signals: high price per square meter with low real activity, developments listed but lacking visible occupancy, or metrics that do not align with each other. We do not say they are "bad investments"; we flag them as zones warranting additional analysis before deciding. Detection is algorithmic and always accompanied by the metrics that triggered the alert.

## How are Strategic Intelligence (IE) scores calculated?

IE scores combine quantitative variables (price, appreciation, amenities, connectivity, demographics) with weightings documented publicly for each indicator. The calculation is reproducible: any user can understand why a neighborhood has a specific score. We publish detailed methodology in the transparency section and update scores periodically as new data arrives.

## Where does the data come from?

We combine multiple sources: public government data (national statistical offices, cadastres, open data portals), market data from commercial partners, real estate listing aggregators, and proprietary models that derive indicators from the above. Each indicator includes its source and update date. We do not use user personal data to build public indices.

## Can I export my data?

Yes. You can request a full export of the data associated with your account (profile, preferences, query history) from user settings or by writing to privacy@desarrollosmx.com. The export is delivered in JSON or CSV format, in accordance with the data portability right recognized under applicable state privacy laws.

## Is my data secure?

We implement encryption in transit and at rest, multi-factor authentication for internal staff, role-based access controls, and regular security audits. Data is hosted on cloud infrastructure with internationally recognized certifications. See the Privacy Policy for complete details on security measures and data handling.

## How do I cancel my account?

From your account settings you can request cancellation at any time. You can also write to support@desarrollosmx.com. Cancellation is final; you will receive email confirmation, and your data enters the retention period described in the Privacy Policy before definitive deletion.

## Will I lose my data if I cancel?

Data associated with your account is retained for a limited period after cancellation (up to 24 months) to fulfill legal and security obligations, after which it is deleted or irreversibly anonymized. If you want to keep your information, we recommend requesting an export before cancelling.

## Do you support countries other than the U.S.?

Yes. The platform supports localization for Mexico, Colombia, Argentina, and Brazil, in addition to U.S.-localized access. Each country has its own data coverage, language (Spanish for MX/CO/AR, Portuguese for Brazil), local currency (MXN, COP, ARS, BRL, USD), and specific privacy jurisdiction. City-level coverage varies based on data availability.

## Do you have a public API?

Public API access is not available in the initial phase (H1). We are designing the API for integrations with commercial partners, real estate portals, and external developers, with launch planned in later phases. If you are interested in early integration, write to partnerships@desarrollosmx.com and we will contact you when the beta program opens.
